EOS: A High-Energy Physics Software for Flavour ObservablesÂ¶

EOS is a software framework for applications in high-energy physics; in particular flavour physics. It is written in C++14, and provides both a C++14 and a Python3 API. It has been authored with three use cases in mind.

  1. Theory predictions and uncertainty estimates of observables and further theoretical quantities in the field of flavor physics. EOS aspires to produce theory estimates and their inherent uncertainties of publication quality, and has produced such estimates in the past.

  2. Parameter Inference based on experimental measurements and/or theoretical constraints. For this use case, EOS defaults to the Bayesian framework of parameter inference. Moreover, EOS provides a database of a large number of experimental measurements and theoretical constraints for immediate use.

  3. Production of pseudo events for a variety of flavor-physics-related processes using Monte Carlo methods.
